Newton Kariuki

Robotics and Robots OS 2 Developer

July 2023 - Present
Project: UFactory XArm7 digital twin

This project focuses on the development of a digital twinning solution for the uFactory xArm7 robotic arm, using ROS 2 and Unity for virtual control and real-time synchronization. The primary goal is to enable seamless bidirectional communication between the real robotic arm and its virtual counterpart in Unity. This allows any movement or action performed by the physical robot to be reflected in the virtual model and vice versa. Key functionalities include control via both joint space and Cartesian space commands, enabling precise manipulation and coordination between the real and virtual environments. The project aims to leverage the digital twin for applications in simulation, remote control, and advanced robotic interaction scenarios, contributing to advancements in robotics, automation, and system testing

Wesley Rotich

Researcher

March 2024 - Present
Project: Human Robot Collaboration

Human-Robot Collaboration (HRC) is being revolutionized by the integration of Augmented Reality (AR) and Machine Learning (ML). AR enhances human-robot interaction by overlaying digital information onto the physical environment, allowing humans to visualize and guide robotic actions in real time. This leads to more intuitive control, safer operations, and hands-free collaboration. Meanwhile, ML enables robots to learn from human behavior, adapt to changing environments, and optimize task performance. The synergy of AR and ML creates smarter, more efficient workspaces, where humans and robots collaborate seamlessly, enhancing productivity across industries like manufacturing, healthcare, and education.
